Introduction

I'm Hristo, a game developer with a BSc in Computer Science. I specialize in creating dynamic, immersive gameplay experiences. With over four years of experience using Unity, I have honed my skills in C# and C++, while currently exploring Unreal Engine through a combination of C++ and blueprints.

My journey into game development has been enriched by my studies at the University of London, where I embraced various technical and creative challenges. I believe in fostering a collaborative learning environment, always striving to uplift my peers and share knowledge to benefit the entire team.

My Skills

Throughout my career, I have developed a broad skill set that encompasses various programming languages and frameworks. My strongest skills include:

  • C# and C++ for game development
  • Expertise in Unity and Unreal Engine, utilizing both C++ and blueprints
  • Version control experience with Git and Plastic

Creating engaging game AI is a core focus of my work. I have implemented FSM and priority-based HFSM to bring intelligent and challenging behaviors to life, especially through adaptive mechanics and dynamic interactions. My passion extends to crafting immersive experiences with responsive controls, fluid animations, and engaging physics interactions.

My Interests

From a young age, I was captivated by game AI, which seemed like black magic that brought games to life. It was the first aspect of development I wanted to understand, and I still love deepening my knowledge in this area. I'm currently reading Game AI Pro, focusing on concepts like Behaviour Trees, GOAP, Utility AI, and HTN.

I see video games as a unique artistic medium and strive to create mechanics that feel intuitive and rewarding to use. My focus on game design includes crafting solid game design documents (GDD) and conducting playtesting sessions to gather user feedback, ensuring that my designs resonate with players.

Dedicated to code quality and optimization, I embrace SOLID principles, frequent refactoring, and debugging sessions. This commitment to clear, maintainable code fosters collaboration and establishes best practices within teams. I'm also enthusiastic about optimizing performance, utilizing profiling tools, and pushing for minimal overhead, especially in creating efficient shaders and managing memory.

My Journey

My love for games started at age 6 when I began emulating retro games. By 15, I was playing titles like Dark Souls, Mount & Blade, Crusader Kings, and The Binding of Isaac, which truly sparked my appreciation for games as an artistic medium. I often watched GDC talks, aspiring to develop the same level of skill and technical mastery as industry professionals.

At 18, during the first COVID lockdown, I developed my first 2D platformer. While working as a technical support analyst, I co-founded Black Sea Trident, a small indie team where we worked on game projects in our spare time. This experience motivated me to leave that job and pursue a BSc in Computer Science, where I gained a deeper understanding of mathematics, physics, OOP, game development, engine architecture, and 3D graphics programming.

Game development has been my career goal from the beginning, and personal projects have taught me invaluable lessons about balancing scope, optimizing systems, and refining gameplay mechanics. I continually push my technical skills through rapid prototyping and mastering new programming techniques to ensure each game delivers satisfying and polished experiences. While I'm a programmer at heart, I hold a special love for game design, art, and sound, as great games are created through love and collaboration across multiple disciplines.
